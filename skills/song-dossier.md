The user is asking for a concise dossier on a Grateful Dead song — its origins,
meaning, and evolution across the band's career.

## What to do

1. Search jerrybase.com for the song's performance history (debut, final, total plays).
2. Search for David Dodd's annotated lyrics and any documented band commentary.
3. Present a tight, information-dense brief on the song.

## Format

Use this structure:

*[Song Title]*
_[Writer(s)] · Debut: [date] · Final: [date] · Played [N] times_

*Origins*
How the song was written, the studio version, and any notable lore. 2-3 sentences.

*What It Means*
The song's themes and meaning — weave together documented interpretations from
Dodd, band interviews, and community consensus. Not verse-by-verse; a coherent
narrative. 3-5 sentences.

*How It Evolved*
Era-by-era sketch of how the song changed live. Focus on the most interesting
transformations — don't cover every year, just the meaningful shifts. 3-5 sentences.

*Worth Hearing*
One or two specific versions to check out, with date and a one-line note on why.

## Rules

- Verify debut/final dates and play count against jerrybase.com
- Song meaning must come from documented sources (Dodd, interviews, liner notes) — not your own literary analysis
- If you can't verify a claim, omit it
- Keep the whole response under 350 words
- Dense and curated, not padded — every sentence earns its place
